About Agriculture

Agriculture is the backbone of human civilization. It involves cultivating plants and rearing animals to produce food, fiber, medicine, and other essential products.

History of Agriculture

Agriculture began over 10,000 years ago when humans shifted from hunting and gathering to farming. Early farmers domesticated crops like wheat and rice and animals such as cattle and sheep, laying the foundation for modern societies.

Importance of Agriculture

  • Ensures food security for growing populations
  • Provides employment to rural and urban communities
  • Supports national and global economies
  • Supplies raw materials for industries

Types of Farming

Traditional Farming

Uses simple tools, local seeds, and natural rainfall. Mostly practiced in rural areas.

Modern Farming

Uses machinery, improved seeds, irrigation, and fertilizers to increase productivity.

Organic Farming

Relies on natural fertilizers and pest control methods to protect soil health.

Mixed Farming

Combines crop cultivation and livestock rearing on the same farm.
